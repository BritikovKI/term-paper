Termination and nontermination analysis are very active areas of research. We will discuss techniques,
related to Interpolation-based Termination Analysis below.

\textbf{Termination.} There exist many different techniques for the termination analysis of software.
One of the most researched and applied techniques iss the synthesis of the ranking functions for the transition
relation, which allows to prove termination~\cite{DBLP:conf/vmcai/PodelskiR04,DBLP:conf/cav/KuraUH20,DBLP:journals/jacm/Ben-AmramG14,DBLP:conf/cav/HeizmannHP14,DBLP:journals/jar/GieslABEFFHOPSS17,DBLP:conf/esop/SaritaSGSV26,DBLP:conf/sas/Ben-AmramDG19}. 
State of the art techniques are centered on the construction of multiphase or lexicographic ranking functions.
These complex techniques significantly broaden the class of programs that ranking functions can handle.
However, since they reason on the original transitions, the complexity of original system can make construction of ranking 
function more difficult.

Another techniques, which allows to efficiently prove termination are fixpoint computation~\cite{DBLP:journals/pacmpl/UnnoTGK23} and 
the computation of closed forms~\cite{DBLP:conf/sas/FrohnHG20,DBLP:journals/fmsd/HarkFG25}.

There also exists another set of techniques which can prove termination that are based on the construction of well-founded transition
invariants~\cite{DBLP:conf/pldi/CookPR06,DBLP:conf/cav/KroeningSTW10,DBLP:conf/tacas/TsitovichSWK11}.
These approaches allow to approximate the behaviour of the transition system, and if the approximation is calculated succesfully the termination analysis
is significantly simplified.
The core problem of these approaches is the synthesis of transition invariant.
Majority of transition invariant-based techniques are based on the synthesis of transition invariants via template synthesis, 
which is not guided by the termination conditions and can often diverge and sometimes even connot produce a descriptive
invariant.

\textbf{Nontermination.} Nontermination analysis is typically centered around the construction of recurrent sets — sets of states from which execution cannot 
escape~\cite{DBLP:conf/sas/Ben-AmramDG19,DBLP:conf/cade/FrohnG22,DBLP:conf/tacas/BrockschmidtCIK16,DBLP:conf/tacas/ChenCFNO14,DBLP:conf/foveoos/BrockschmidtSOG11,DBLP:conf/cav/LarrazNORR14}.
These techniques can vary from the template-based synthesis to the safety-check based recurrent set construction.
Another set of techniques are based on the safety-based termination analysis~\cite{DBLP:journals/entcs/BiereAS02,DBLP:conf/tacas/UrbanGK16},
that allow to use safety provers for the proof of nontermination. 
An alternative family of techniques detects nontermination by identifying 
lasso-shaped execution, finite paths leading to an infinite loop~\cite{DBLP:journals/entcs/BiereAS02,DBLP:conf/tacas/LeikeH18}.
