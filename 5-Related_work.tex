\section{Related Work}
\label{Sec:Related}

\textbf{Invariant generation.} Inductive invariant generation is a widely used technique for 
reasoning about loops by producing an overapproximation of program states, 
such that any loop iteration remains within the same overapproximation. 
Multiple approaches include CEGAR and predicate 
abstraction~\cite{DBLP:conf/fmcad/HojjatR18,mcmillan2013computing}, 
IC3/PDR~\cite{DBLP:journals/fmsd/KomuravelliGC16}, 
program transformation-based methods~\cite{DBLP:conf/cav/KafleGM16}, 
syntax-guided synthesis~\cite{DBLP:journals/fmsd/FedyukovichKB20}, and machine learning/neural 
networks~\cite{DBLP:conf/iclr/RyanWYGJ20,DBLP:journals/corr/abs-2311-07948}. 
One of the most popular approaches, used in a wide variety of algorithms, is 
interpolation-based invariant generation~\cite{DBLP:conf/cav/McMillan06,DBLP:conf/kbse/LinSXLSH17,DBLP:conf/fmcad/BlichaFHS22,mcmillan2013computing,DBLP:conf/cav/McMillan03,DBLP:journals/fmsd/KomuravelliGC16,DBLP:conf/fmcad/HojjatR18}. 
A recent advancement in this direction is Multiloop TPA~\cite{DBLP:conf/fm/BritikovBSF24}, which 
specifically targets multi-loop programs. 
However, unlike \solveCHC, this method does not support software with nested loops or gotos 
and cannot be applied to general linear CHC systems.

\textbf{Single-loop transformation.} A common strategy for analyzing complex 
systems is to transform them into a single-loop representation~\cite{DBLP:journals/fmsd/DonaldsonKR11,DBLP:books/aw/AhoSU86,bueno2020horn2vmt}. 
This enables the application of techniques such as IMC or TPA, optimized for 
single-loop analysis~\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/abs-2208-05046}, allowing them 
to reason over the original complex structure.

\textbf{Loop acceleration.} Techniques~\cite{DBLP:conf/cav/BozgaIK10,DBLP:journals/sttt/BardinFLP08,DBLP:conf/tacas/Frohn20,DBLP:conf/cade/FrohnG22,DBLP:journals/corr/abs-1905-11187,DBLP:conf/fm/FrohnG24} 
aim to produce quantifier-free first-order formulas that are equivalent to or underapproximate a loop's behaviour. 
These methods are motivated by improving the scalability of safety verification, attempting to replace a 
loop with a concrete formula. 
State-of-the-art acceleration approaches predominantly rely on underapproximation and can struggle 
with proving safety.

\textbf{Loop summarization.} A group of approaches overapproximating loop execution 
by constructing a symbolic relation between loop inputs and outputs, allowing the loop to be 
abstracted away in subsequent analysis. 
Common methods include symbolic abstraction~\cite{DBLP:conf/sac/BlichaKT22,DBLP:conf/cav/SilvermanK19,DBLP:conf/atva/KroeningSTTW08} 
and symbolic execution~\cite{DBLP:conf/issta/StrejcekT12,DBLP:journals/tse/XieCZLLL19,DBLP:conf/issta/GodefroidL11}. 
However, such approaches are typically property-agnostic and unaware of the surrounding system, 
which can reduce their effectiveness. 
Some of the approaches above also do not support summarization of nested loops. 
In contrast, our method performs property-guided summary generation and handles nested loops.
